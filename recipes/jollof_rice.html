Jollof Rice

Desciption

Jollof Rice is a vibrant and aromatic West African dish renowned for its vibrant red hue and bold flavors. It's made by simmering long-grain rice in a fragrant tomato-based sauce infused with spices like thyme, curry, and scotch bonnet peppers. This one-pot wonder can be customized with your choice of protein and vegetables, offering a tantalizing taste of West African cuisine that's both comforting and flavorful.


Ingrdients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced (optional)
  • 1 can (14 oz) of diced tomatoes or 3-4 fresh tomatoes, blended
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet or habanero peppers (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or cayenne pepper (optional, for added heat)
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon white or black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon bouillon powder or cube (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 cups chicken or vegetable broth or water
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., peas, carrots, green beans; optional)
  • Protein of your choice (e.g., chicken, shrimp, or tofu; optional)

Steps

Prepare the Ingredients

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in cold water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. If using chicken, shrimp, or tofu, season and cook them separately until they are fully cooked. Set aside.

Prepare the Jollof Sauce

  1. In a blender, combine the diced tomatoes, scotch bonnet peppers, and bouillon powder or cube (if using). Blend until smooth.

Cook the Jollof Rice

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and ginger (if using) and sauté for another minute or until fragrant.
  4. Add the finely chopped red and green bell peppers and continue to sauté until they soften.
  5. Pour in the blended tomato mixture and stir well. Allow it to cook down for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate from the tomato mixture.
  6. Season the sauce with thyme, curry powder, paprika or cayenne pepper (for heat), white or black pepper, and salt to taste. Adjust the seasoning to your preference.
  7. Add the drained rice to the pot and stir to coat it evenly with the sauce.
  8. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth (or water) to cover the rice completely. Add mixed vegetables (if using).
  9. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 25-30 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed the liquid. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  10. If you cooked protein earlier, add it back to the pot during the last 5 minutes of cooking to heat through.

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish, and it can be customized with your choice of protein or additional vegetables to suit your taste. Enjoy!
